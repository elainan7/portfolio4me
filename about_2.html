I'm a recent summa cum laude graduate from Seattle University with a degree in Communication and Media — specializing in Strategic Communication with a minor in Philosophy. I'm at the beginning of my career and genuinely excited about the work ahead.

What drives me is the space where creativity and strategy overlap. I like asking why something works, not just making it look good. I've spent the past few years building things — campaigns, teams, communities — and learning what it actually takes to connect with people.

Outside of work, I care deeply about animal welfare, sustainable food systems, community-building, and the kind of communication that treats people as thinking humans rather than passive consumers.

01

Volunteer Trip
to England

"Whether I'm hand-churning homemade ice cream or learning from a new community, I bring curiosity, intention, and heart to everything I do."

After graduating early from Seattle University, I used my final quarter as a chance to do something meaningful before entering the working world. I traveled solo to England to volunteer on a slaughter-free dairy farm through WWOOF.

For five weeks, I lived and worked alongside people from a wide range of cultures, nationalities, and religions — united by a shared love for animals, ethical farming, and the peaceful countryside. The farm sold raw milk from happy cows to customers across London, and its commitment to ethical dairy aligned perfectly with my values.

This experience pushed me far outside my comfort zone and taught me how to connect and collaborate with people from all walks of life — skills I carry into every team I work with.

02

Founding the SU
Women's Soccer Club

"The more community you build around a shared purpose, the more sustainable that purpose becomes."

When I arrived at Seattle University, the Women's Club Soccer team had completely dissolved. As someone who values both competitive play and community, I took it upon myself to rebuild it from the ground up.

I navigated the university's administrative process to reestablish the club, collaborated with the URec marketing team to create flyers and digital content, and recruited players through email campaigns and face-to-face outreach. What started as a vision is now a vibrant team of 18 dedicated athletes and over 40 club members.

Over two years, we've competed in tournaments in Ellensburg and Corvallis, partnered with the Seattle Reign, and volunteered at youth soccer camps. Leading through uncertainty while wearing many hats taught me how powerful passion can be when it's met with hard work.
